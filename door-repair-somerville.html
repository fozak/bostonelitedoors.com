About This Project

This Somerville homeowner had a 90-year-old hardwood entry door that had seen better days — the sill had rotted through, the original mortise hardware was seized solid, and years of paint buildup had completely hidden the wood grain beneath. Replacement wasn't on the table; the goal was to save the door and restore it to its original character.

We replaced the rotted sill and repaired the lower jamb, stripped the door down to bare wood, patched damaged panel areas, and fully restored the original mortise hardware. A penetrating oil finish and spar varnish topcoat were applied throughout. New weatherstripping on all four sides and a door sweep complete the seal — the door closes tight and will stand up to Boston winters.

Work Completed
  • Rotted sill removed and replaced — lower jamb repaired
  • Full chemical and mechanical paint strip to bare wood
  • Damaged panel sections filled and faired
  • Original mortise lock set cleaned, lubricated, and restored
  • Hinges re-mortised and re-hung with stainless screws
  • Penetrating oil finish + spar varnish topcoat applied
  • Full weatherstrip replacement — head, jambs, and sweep
  • Door re-aligned in frame, smooth operation confirmed
