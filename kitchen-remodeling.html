The Kitchen Is the Most Important Room In Your Boston Home

Greater Boston's housing market is highly competitive. Buyers walk through kitchens first — and dated cabinets, old appliances, and poor layouts are the fastest way to lose a sale or a refinance appraisal. A remodeled kitchen is one of the few home improvements that pays back close to what it costs.

Why Greater Boston homeowners remodel their kitchens now
  • Pre-1990 kitchens are the #1 reason Boston-area homes sit on the market longer

  • Laminate countertops and oak cabinets drop appraised value in this market
  • Older kitchens often have undersized electrical panels and no dedicated appliance circuits — a code issue and a safety issue
What a kitchen remodel returns in Greater Boston
  • Minor kitchen remodel returns 80–85% of cost at sale — one of the highest ROIs in home improvement

Common Questions

Kitchen remodels in Greater Boston typically range from $15,000 for a mid-range refresh — new cabinets, countertops, hardware, and paint — to $60,000+ for a full gut renovation with custom cabinetry, high-end appliances, and a layout change. We provide a written, itemized estimate after the in-home walkthrough. No vague "starting from" quotes — you see exactly where every dollar goes before any work begins.

Most full kitchen remodels take 3–6 weeks from demo to final walkthrough. Simple updates — cabinet refacing, new countertops, backsplash — can run 1–2 weeks. Projects involving structural wall removal or full layout changes take 5–8 weeks. We give you a firm schedule before work begins and update you weekly — no weeks of silence while your kitchen is torn apart.

In Massachusetts, permits are required for any work involving electrical changes (new circuits, panel upgrades, relocated outlets), plumbing (moving a sink, adding a dishwasher line), or structural work (wall removal, beam installation). Cosmetic-only updates — painting, new hardware, replacing appliances in the same location — typically don't require permits. We evaluate every project and pull all required permits before demo begins. Unpermitted electrical and plumbing work is a real liability at resale — we never skip this step.
