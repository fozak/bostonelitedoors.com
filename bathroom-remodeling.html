Bathroom Renovations in Greater Boston Require the Right Approach

New England's climate puts real stress on bathrooms — freeze-thaw cycles, humidity, and aging housing stock create problems that a surface-level refresh won't fix. We build bathrooms to handle Boston winters and Boston summers, not just look good on day one.

What we see in Greater Boston bathrooms
  • Failed waterproofing behind tile — common in pre-1990 bathrooms, causes rot and mold inside walls
  • Undersized ventilation fans that don't meet Massachusetts code — accelerates mold growth
  • Original cast-iron plumbing in older homes requiring updates before tile work begins
  • Missing GFCI protection near water sources — a code violation and an insurance issue
  • Cold tile floors in winter — heated floor systems are a worthwhile upgrade in this climate
What a renovated bathroom delivers
  • Updated bathrooms add $10,000–$25,000 to appraised value in the Boston metro
  • Master bath renovations are the second most impactful project for resale after kitchens
  • Proper waterproofing prevents $20,000+ in structural damage over time
  • Financing available — spread the cost of a full renovation over time

Common Questions

Bathroom renovations in Greater Boston typically start at $8,000 for a focused update — new tile, vanity, and fixtures in an existing layout. Full gut renovations with custom tile, frameless glass, and a new layout run $18,000–$40,000 for a standard full bath. Luxury master bathrooms with freestanding tubs, heated floors, and high-end fixtures can reach $50,000+. We provide a written, itemized estimate after the in-home walkthrough — no surprises mid-project.

Most full bathroom renovations take 2–3 weeks from demo to final fixtures. Larger master baths with custom tile work and freestanding tubs can run 4–5 weeks. Simple updates — vanity swap, new tile, fixture replacement without moving plumbing — can be done in under a week. We give you a firm schedule before work begins and stick to it.

In Massachusetts, permits are required whenever plumbing is relocated (moving a toilet, sink, or shower drain), electrical work is done (adding circuits, relocating outlets, installing a new exhaust fan with its own circuit), or structural changes are made. Cosmetic-only work — replacing a vanity in the same location, swapping fixtures, retiling over existing substrate — typically doesn't require a permit. We evaluate every project and pull all required permits before demo. Unpermitted plumbing and electrical work creates liability at resale and can void homeowner's insurance claims.

Greater Boston's climate — hot humid summers and cold winters — puts real stress on bathroom waterproofing. Tile alone is not waterproof. Without a proper waterproof membrane behind the tile on shower walls and floors, water migrates into the framing and causes rot, mold, and structural damage that can cost $10,000–$30,000 to remediate. We use cement board and RedGard or Schluter Kerdi systems on every shower and wet area — not just in the visible spots.

Yes — tub-to-shower conversions are one of our most requested bathroom projects in Greater Boston. We remove the tub, reframe as needed, install a linear or center drain, apply full waterproofing, and tile the shower floor and walls. We can add a frameless glass enclosure, built-in niche, bench, and rainfall or handheld showerhead. Note: if your home has only one bathtub, removing it can affect resale value in the family-home segment — we'll discuss this honestly during the estimate.
