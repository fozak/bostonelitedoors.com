Door Repair Services

Every common door problem — and the uncommon ones too.

Sticking & Binding Doors

Seasonal swelling, settling foundations, and paint buildup all cause doors to stick or bind. We plane, shave, and re-hang for a smooth, gap-free fit that lasts.

Sagging & Misaligned Doors

Worn hinges, stripped screw holes, and shifting frames knock doors out of square. We reset hinges, fill and re-fasten with structural screws, and realign the door in its frame.

Frame & Jamb Repair

Rotted, cracked, or broken door frames repaired or replaced — including split jambs, kicked-in frames after forced entry, and water-damaged wood around exterior doors.

Hinge Replacement & Repair

Squeaking, loose, or broken hinges replaced with the correct grade hardware. Stripped hinge mortises rebuilt with wood filler and re-cut for a solid, lasting mount.

Strike Plate & Latch Repair

Latches that don't catch, strike plates that are misaligned, and doors that won't stay closed. Adjusted, reinforced, or replaced — with security-grade strike plates available.

Panel & Glass Repair

Cracked or damaged door panels repaired or replaced. Broken glass in doors and sidelights replaced — including insulated glass units, decorative glass, and safety glass.

Repair vs. Replace — Honest Advice

Most door problems don't require a new door. A sticking door is rarely a replacement job — it's a 30-minute fix. But some doors have reached end of life, and we'll tell you honestly which is which before we quote.

Repair usually makes sense when:
  • The door is solid wood and structurally sound
  • It's a historic or custom door worth preserving
  • The problem is mechanical — hinges, latch, alignment
  • Damage is isolated (one panel, one corner, one edge)
Replacement makes sense when:
  • Rot or water damage has compromised the core
  • The door is a hollow-core exterior door (security risk)
  • The frame and door together are beyond repair
  • Repair cost approaches replacement cost
